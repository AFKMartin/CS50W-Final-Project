Riemann Zeta Function

The Riemann zeta function ζ(s) is initially defined for real s > 1 by the series ζ(s) = Σ 1 / n^s. The famous Riemann Hypothesis concerns the zeros of its analytic continuation in the complex plane, which are conjectured to lie on the critical line Re(s) = 1/2.

This plot shows an approximation of the Riemann zeta function for real values of s > 1.
